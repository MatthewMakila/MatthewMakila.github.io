About Me

I am passionate about human-centered computing, with a focus on improving social outcomes and technology for Black, Latin, and neurodiverse communities. My research explores how social conditions shape our lived experiences, while seeking to create tools and strategies that enhance how marginalized groups engage with and benefit from technology.

I look to utilize data science, qualitative methods, and design principles in my research to combat social inequities.

Eventually, I hope to become a principal investigator of my own lab, creating a safe space for marginalized people to study social phenomena affecting marginalized people! One of my biggest priorities is to show my community they have a place in STEM.

Outside of research, I love to be outside, so you'll often find me hiking, exploring cities, trying new foods, thrifting, and playing a lot of tennis.

Ongoing Work

Journaling Documentation & Customization Practices

In collaboration with the Design Tools Lab at Barnard College, I am helping run user studies and develop a digital application for journaling with close friends and partners at long distance!

Accessible learning tools for K-8 and university students

This project uses a series of curated games to teach principles of accessibility and its importance in design to younger children and adults (K-8 and university). There are games focused on visual disabilities, physical disabilities, neurodiverse and cognitive disabilities, and more!

Older Adults' Financial Literacy and Management Practices

We ran a series of interviews to understand financial literacy and financial management practices for older adults. With these insights, we plan to develop a tool that bridges gaps in accessibility regarding receiving quality financial decision-making advice.

Choices About Community Safety Against the Backdrop of Police Surveillance

Often times, communities face one-dimensional spiels about safety from local leadership that primarily advertise police surveillance and technology as the solution to crime (ignoring root, social causes). This research experiments with a presenting community members a wider range of choices, with hopes to understand how choices regarding safety are impacted by the presentation of given options.
